### Wagering Requirements Explained

In simple terms, a wagering requirement is the number of times you need to bet your bonus amount before you can withdraw any associated winnings. For instance, if you receive a £100 bonus with a 10x wagering requirement, you must wager £1,000 before making a withdrawal. This ensures that the bonus is used for its intended purpose: to engage with the casino's offerings.

### How Wagering Requirements Work in Practice

## Types of Wagering Requirements

There are several types of wagering requirements that players might encounter. Understanding these can help you choose the best bonuses to suit your gaming style.

### Bonus-Only Wagering

This type of requirement means you only need to wager the bonus amount a certain number of times. It is generally more favourable for players as the wagering is limited to the bonus amount. This can make it easier to meet the requirements and withdraw any winnings.

### Deposit + Bonus Wagering

Here, the requirement applies to both the deposit and the bonus. For example, if you deposit £50 and receive a £50 bonus, with a 20x wagering requirement, you must wager £2,000 (20 x £100). This type of requirement can be more challenging, as it involves a larger total wagering amount.

### Free Spin Wagering

Free spin winnings often come with their own set of requirements. These winnings may need to be wagered a specific number of times before they can be withdrawn. Understanding the terms associated with free spins is essential to maximize their value.

## Calculating Wagering Requirements

Understanding how to calculate wagering requirements can help you better manage your casino experience. Here’s a simple guide:

1. **Determine the Requirement**: Identify whether the requirement applies to the bonus, deposit, or both. This will dictate the total amount you need to wager.
2. **Calculate the Total Wagering Amount**: Multiply the bonus (and deposit, if applicable) by the wagering requirement. For example, a £50 bonus with a 20x requirement means you need to wager £1,000.
3. **Consider Game Contributions**: Different games contribute differently to wagering requirements. For instance, slots might contribute 100%, while table games might contribute less. This means that playing slots could help you meet the requirements faster.
